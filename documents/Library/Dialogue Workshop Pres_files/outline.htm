  • A Dialogue Workshop

Central Features of
the Dialogue Workshop
  • Team sets its own agenda (“what”)
  • Takes responsibility for “how” it is working
  • Help from external facilitator
  • Enabling…
    • Communication to become unusually direct, clear and complete
    • A meeting of minds
10
Always amazing?
  • Never entirely comfortable or perfect
  • Magical moments when blocks shift
  • But not a magic cure-all
  • Issues raised require further work
  • Difficult issues need time and concentration
  • Slipping back happens
11
Key Facilitator Interventions
  • Time-out
  • Range of possibilities, experiment as needed, not fixed approach or dry theory
  • Examples of experiments:
    • dealing with head to head conflict
    • truth-telling with MD who fell asleep
12
Positive Outcomes because…
  • Different than run of the mill
    • Honesty, because everything is noticed
    • Freely opening hidden issues
    • High levels of respect and support
    • No table, no papers, circle of chairs
    • Off-site
    • No distractions
    • Deeply human
