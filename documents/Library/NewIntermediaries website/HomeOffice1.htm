Creating An Appetite for Change
Who?
Fiona Coffey and Tony Page working with Tony Williams, Sue Street and 180 managers and staff within a directorate of the Home Office.

What is the box?
The Home Office had made a commitment to achieve an unprecendented reduction in the rate of reoffending
Traditionally Home Office people are intelligent and extremely busy, working long hours in their own separate units, with little lateral contact, advising ministers and dealing with daily cirses from parliament and press.
Delivering the reoffending target will not be achieved unless people adopt new ways of working using project management.

Thinking outside the box?
This is more complex and difficult than many realise and no single person in the criminal justice system knows how to do it. It's a long haul.
Many modernisation projects are underway. Previously many have failed.
Let's mobilise everyone in thinking about the problem before rushing into solutions

What was the process?
Informal exploratory meetings with director and internal consultant
"Over the wall" field visits to police, prisons, probation, magistrates ...
2 day off-site top team workshop and follow-up meetings
Large group meetings for staff with visitors from criminal justice system
Combined meetings for top team with delegates from large group meetings
Unit meetings
Handover meeting
Results?
Formal objectives and terms of reference for phase 1 accomplished
Most directorate staff and 40 outside visitors attended workshops
Significant resistance from change weary and cynical staff and various major concerns surfaced
Forthright discussions at Combined Meetings produced agreement by top team to address concerns particularly over unfilled vacancies
Staff identified how they will adopt new ways of working both inside the directorate and in the field, and the new IT support they need
Targets set for phase 2. The momentum for change exists and the directorate is proceeding in phase 2 as intended without outside help.
