Dear all,

Following up on the NI forum theatre workshop earlier this week, I promised

a reference to drama theory. Here it is:

Nigel Howard, Confrontation Analysis: How to win operations other than war,

CCRP Publication series, 1999. This is an application of drama theory to

military peace-keeping operations, as in the former Yugoslavia. It is

freely available, and can be ordered from the US Dept of Defense

Cooperative Research Program site www.dodccrp.org - or can be downloaded

from there in pdf format if you prefer.

I've also attached a brief article from New Scientist on drama theory. Bear

with me on this one. At the beginning it looks like yet another, sterile

piece on game theory - but stick with it until the story, about halfway

down, of what happened when two economists disputed the price of the

taxi-ride from the airport to their hotel in Jerusalem.

As I mentioned during the workshop, drama theory has already been quite

widely applied, not only in military or political scenarios, but also in

relation to mergers & acquisitions and complex, multi-agency conflict (eg,

in the health service).

The point is not only that drama theory complements (and extends) forum

theatre, but more that to a potential sponsor of a change initiative that

includes some element of forum theatre, it could provide a legitimating (=

reassuring and de-risking) analytical foundation.

I do have more material here so if your curious do get in touch.
