THINKING ROUNDS

 

Application:

 

•   Whenever you want involvement and commitment to harness the thinking of a group quickly

•   When you want a team/group to listen well and encourage a balanced contribution from everyone

•   When you want to include quieter members of a team/group or limit over contribution from “noisier” members

•   When you want people, regardless of status levels, to have equal say

 

 

How to do this:

 

•   Ask group to sit in circles where they can see each other (works best with 3-5 people in each circle)

•   Have a fiipchart or a notepad nearby to capture the thoughts. No writing should take place during the thinking rounds themselves

•   Write the process up on a flipchart for all to see

 

Process

 

Before you begin you will need to agree:

 

•   Who will keep time ( using a watch with a second hand or kitchen timer )

•   Who will speak first, second and third etc

 

Round One

 

In this round each speaker, in turn, has two minutes to state their ideas on the topic without interruption or discussion. The other members of the group should give the speaker good attention. New speakers can build on previous ideas or can say “pass”.

 

Round Two

 

Each person in turn, has one minute to speak without interruption with a view to building on the thinking shared in Round One. This is a chance to build on each other’s ideas, to explore creative options and state any new ideas or combinations of ideas. You can say “pass” if you have nothing more to add.

 

Round Three

 

Each person has two minutes to state what they believe is the best thinking generated by the group and this is recorded on flip chart paper.

 

Round Four

 

The group has six minutes to discuss and attempt to reach a consensus. The group then records their ideas on flip chart paper and should be ready to share their views within the whole group.

Leadership In a Thinking Environment

 

Action is only as good as the thinking behind it.

Thinking is only as good as the quality of people’s attention for each other.

 

 

Ten Components of a Thinking Environment

 

The quality ofyour attention determines the quality of other people’s thinking.

 

 

 

1.   Attention -- Listening with sustained interest and respect.- and without interruption

 

 

 

2.   Ease -- Offering freedom from rush or urgency

 

 

3.   Appreciation -- Practising a ten-to-one ratio of appreciation to criticism

 

 

 

4.     Encouragement -- Going beyond competition

 

 

 

5.   Equality -- Focusing on each other as thinking peers Giving equal turns and attention

Keeping agreements and boundaries

 

 

 

6.     Diversity -- Adding quality by celebrating the differences between us

 

 

7.     Feelings -- Allowing sufficient emotional release to restore thinking

 

 

8.   Place -- Creating a physical environment that says back to people, ‘You matter.’

 

 

9.   Information -- Providing a full and accurate picture of reality

 

 

10. Incisive Questions -- Removing assumptions that limit ideas
