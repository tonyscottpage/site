Philip Gibson

What is Philip’s exciting new internet project which is ‘close to his heart and very exciting? Is he still planning to work in corporate communications? What does taking an indefinite sabbatical from Greenly’s mean?

In his own words

"I've had and continue to have 'artistic' leanings.

During the '70s I worked as an expert for Sotheby's the auctioneers -

my area of expertise was 19th century European silver and objects of

vertu (small cabinet objects).

 

I left Sotheby's in 1981 to do a two-year MBA at London Business

School after which I spent a year working as a freelance market

researcher / management consultant. I then joined Wolff Olins,

Europe's leading corporate identity consultancy during which time I

helped organisations think about how they presented themselves, using

graphic design as a way of communicating strategy.

 

I left Wolff Olins ten years ago when our daughter was born. I wanted

to work from home and I've been doing so ever since. During that time

I've worked with various design agencies, I've written corporate

brochures, worked on websites and generally done a bit of this and a

bit of that.

 

Over the past six months or so I've spent almost all of my time on an

Internet start-up, http://www.expertgardener.com. It combines two of

my interests - the Internet and gardening. Do take a look at it and

in particular visit one of the five communities we've been fostering.

Their success is one of the reasons I'm keen that the New

Intermediaries should try out something along those lines."
