Some thoughts on leadership

 

·        Leaders need to define the direction or the “WHAT” but should leave the “HOW” up to the people on the ground

·        Leaders need to give people a chance to influence the outcome

·        The way leaders articulate change is critical

·        Leaders don’t need to know or define everything – they need to share responsibility for developing solutions

·        Leaders need to be open about their own experience

·        As change consultants we need to live with the same things that the change leaders go through

·        Leaders should not withhold difficult information, even though they may be worried about how to communicate it or try to work out all the details before communicating. Usually this leaks out anyway and makes the situation worse

·        Uncertainty is the worst thing. Need to understand where people have invested their security

 

Our stab at the process of change

 

  1. Clarify the vision. Purpose / objectives. Need to contextualise this change with everything else going on
  2. Test commitment at the top level. Need to know as consultants whether to walk away at this stage
  3. Start to build commitment at top level. Conduct stakeholder analysis. Test where there is commitment and engage with important stakeholders
  4. Develop a change strategy / communication strategy.
  5. Articulate your direction. Benchmark where you are now and define how you will track progress
  6. Start to engage people in process. Start conversations. Explore with people what success looks like.

 

Change will be iterative – like a spiral. Interaction / conversation will start to catalyse change with people, who will make small changes, which will lead to further conversation

 

 

Some final thoughts on the process of change

·        You need people who are change leaders at all levels in the organisation

·        Not every change will go the same. There is not a fixed plan or stages

·        Planning change should NOT involve Gantt charts, rather it is about sensing and responding. Planning is around structuring engagement with the business

·        Communication is not just the “communication” stream. Everything done in the name of the change programme is communication (e.g. e-mails to staff  / project meetings)

·        Need to be prepared to modify the change or the pace of change based on feedback from engagement

·        Best communication of change is to live the change. Give people time to get used to change (Which is uncomfortable at first). 50 days to get used to change
