You have two cows.

 

You sell three of them to your publicly listed company, using letters of credit opened by your  brother-in-law at the bank, then execute a debt/equity swap with an  associated general offer so that you get all four cows back, with a tax exemption for five cows.

 

The milk rights of the six cows are transferred via an intermediary to a Cayman Island company secretly owned by the  majority shareholder who sells the rights to all seven cows back to your listed company.

 

The annual report says the company owns eight cows, with an option on one more.

 

When the sham is finally uncovered, you and the other executives siphon off all the companies assets, freeze the assets of the peons working on the farm, and then tell them that there is no use crying over spilled milk.
