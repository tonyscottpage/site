Facilitating voices in conflict – learning points

 

Forcing conflict important in some circumstances

Generally not productive – run and hide

Gently remove

 

Own the dead moose

Conflict exists. Needs supporting. It’s not a boxing ring however.

Overt vs covert conflict – give opportunity to voice – safe environment

Holding everyone equal and human – gently take to the truth

Emotions, everyone valid

Timing and pacing

Get beyond my personal need for harmony

I wonder about the purpose of ritualising conflict – cleansing

“Container” – keep them in the room – dynamic naturally evolves, the moose is owned by everyone

Don’t get caught on a crusade, seeing people as victims

Respect process and respect each other

Arsenal FC – cleansing effect of hearing voice being heard

Oversimplifying – is everyone voicing?

Jo’s story: conflict resolution in School – exploring each person’s feelings and needs leads to trust. No judgements or generalisations/translations process.

What contribution is sought from a facilitator when an organisation is fragmenting under pressure? (School simulation)

 

Middle Managers

A structure (of conversations) to enable

Talking and sharing with safety

An outside view on roles

 

Front liners

Help us get clear on what we really want / need

Step us out of victim role

Wake up my sense of power and respect

 

Leadership

Wake up call – tell us teachers (front liners’) needs / challenge us to take power

Identify options for better ways of working

Sort out the School – solve the problem for us

Offer observations – distance / map the system

Bring the anxiety level down

Focus on the future

Propose actions to sort it out
