Workshop: Facilitating the Inner Voice

______________________________________

 

                             

Julia Williams                                               Tony Page

 

This workshop enables a facilitator to get in touch with the fundamentals of their role as well as extend themselves into some exciting new areas.

 

If you remind yourself how it feels to get something off your chest, then the benefits of voicing become self-evident. Voicing creates a sense of ease and fulfilment, of being true to yourself through being heard and claiming your place in the world.

 

But why might Voicing be relevant to facilitators working in and with organisations?

 

No facilitator would deny the importance of “listening” but the most perceptive amongst us honour an ancient and natural process of “voicing” to navigate really hot and edgy situations such as: 

-        When participants in a meeting challenge what is happening

-        When hot (gender, ethnic, religious, power, harassment) issues surface

-        When trust has broken down and neglected relationships needs to be renewed

-        When something is being avoided, energy is low and no one wants to talk about it

-        When a new partnership needs to be forged under difficult circumstances

 

Hot situations are becoming commonplace, but when mishandled are breaking people and teams apart. With rising pressure in the workplace people are feeling more isolated, hitting more barriers as they try to make even simple things happen. Against this background, what can the facilitator and leader do to enable people, teams, organisations, networks and partnerships to be their best?

 

Julia Williams brings a deep understanding of the energies which move between people as they interact. She shows people how to remove fear and speak from the heart. Tony Page facilitates meetings with all kinds of people and organisations. He shows people how to collaborate. Read his recent article: “Voice Arising”. Together they explore and demonstrate a timeless “voicing” process which transforms the way difficult work gets done.

 

Their workshop gives the facilitator direct experience both of “finding their voice” and “facilitating voices through conflict”. Practical activities uncover the fuller implications of this viewpoint. Moving beyond conventional notions of work, we look at organisations as a series of relationships, conversations and voices out of which motivated work arises. We re-frame the role of the leaders to enable people to step up to their creative, learning edge and thus leverage the performance of their organisations.

 

Through participating in this day we invite you to redefine your role and way of contributing to your organisation.

 

Who for?

Facilitators and leaders wanting to engage their people’s hearts and minds, from any organisation: private sector, public sector, third sector, large or small.

 

Objectives

You will have the opportunity to

-        Tune into the ancient process of “voicing”

-        Experience yourself speaking from head and from heart and notice the difference

-        Find your voice and expand your listening

-        Notice the voice patterns in a group and facilitate voices through conflict

-        Explore the implications: what this reveals about your organisation , leadership and real work

-        Re-frame your role and way of contributing to your organisation.

 

Style of the workshop

The workshop releases a range of different energies including fun, laughter and relaxation! Exercises, designed to be suitable for all, include forthright and heartfelt speaking, listening, physical movement, role playing, observation and reflection.

 

These workshops draw from the direct experience of both participants and tutors, creating a live and ongoing inquiry, and extending our knowledge about human potential in organisation.

Rough timings and session descriptions appear below

0900 Arrival & setting the scene – bringing voices into the room

Participants join the circle, are welcomed, invited to remove their organisational masks and each become present as a person with a voice, engage with the workshop objectives and groundrules, sharing their experiences and questions such as:

-        hot situations – where you feel challenged as a facilitator

-        your organisation today: who gets their voice heard, who doesn’t, the risks attached to speaking your mind, what gets held-in or lost…

 

1000 Experiencing your voice - the feeling of integrity

Julia leads a mini-workshop heightening awareness of your voice energies including 4 core emotions, 4 significant parts of the body, how to manage your fear. Pairs work lets you experience what happens to your voice under conditions of fear, then finding your voice, speaking from your heart and what integrity feels like.

 

1115 Break

 

1130 Noticing voices in the group - the sound of integrity

Julia and Tony co-facilitate a second mini-workshop in which you become more attuned to others and a sense of the group starts to crystallise. We explore levels of “listening”, the shapes of energy fields (prickly, rounded, rising, falling, pushing out, drawing in…), hunter-gatherer traditions of giving voice, 4 dialogue moves, blindfold dialogue, archetypal voices.

 

We begin to notice what’s not being said here, what voices are not being heard, what differences are emerging, the role of the facilitator both in and at the boundary of the group, and what integrity sounds like in the group.

 

1245 Lunch

 

1330 Facilitating voices in conflict

Tony and Julia co-facilitate a third mini-workshop in which emerging differences/ voices in the group are explored, revealing the barriers to voicing which create splitting both inside and between people, giving rise to conflict. The held-in voice is first identified individually and then worked in subgroups, creating the opportunity in trios to facilitate a conflict resolution process.

 

1500 Break

 

1515 Facilitating…continued

The whole group reconvenes in a circle to gather experiences and learning.

 

1545 Releasing human potential in the organisation

Tony leads the group to explore the implications of the voice process for how we work, organise, lead and facilitate. The group simulates the dynamics in a threatened, fearful organisation: “I’m doing my job - what about you?” in which by identifying and removing hidden barriers we re-frame the work of the leader and your own contribution as a facilitator.

 

1700 Close
