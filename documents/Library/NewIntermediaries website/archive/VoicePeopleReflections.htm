Reflections on Voice Dialogue Day

20th May 2002

Hampton Wick Cricket Club

__________________________________

 

 

"Thank you for this inspiring and beautiful day. I was particularly impressed by Esther's gentleness and humanity. I think we were all pretty terrified of the voices within and what might be exposed in front of others, but Esther treated all our voices with so much respect and kindness and we went away knowing each other a lot better, and realising that we all had a pretty similar cast of characters within. For me it was valuable personally and I have since been very aware of the two opposing bits of me pulling in different direction. I have already started using it with one of my clients which has produced some very fascinating material and I can see great potential for using it in a business setting."

 

__________________________________

 

This day impacted on me on many levels: intellectually, emotionally, personally and professionally.  It is my intention to contact Esther to do some more work on a 1;1 basis.

 

The appreciative and respectful space she created was experienced by me as a powerful place for change. I am not sure I got to grips with the theory and some of the distinctions that were being drawn but that in no way undermined the value. On reflection the word Acceptance comes up for me. I feel lighter around the issue that I was reporting on and sense there is something around integrating the strong with the vulnerable which will create a better space for me to connect with others from…. 

 

…may look like hocus pocus in black and white but feels like a real shift another way of knowing, hence my interest in re-engaging with voice dialogue.

  

I am struck that a group of strangers felt able to get stuck in as quickly as we did. Esther and Jennifer held our vulnerability in a way that liberated us and encouraged exploration. I compare this to my recent experience on the other workshops where fear held us up and progress felt tortuous at  times. Somehow with voice dialogue the power lies with the individual with the facilitator supporting the choices the individual is making moment by moment

__________________________________

 

I enjoyed Monday.  I think this enjoyment was about coming together with a group of people I didn't know, who were not trying to further their own agendas but willing to trust each other and explore something new.

 

I think Esther enabled this through the medium of voice dialogue.  The method I found interesting and I admired her skill as a practitioner.  (didn't have any opportunity to work with Jen)

 

I don't think it is a method I would look to use except for/on myself.  By this I mean reflecting on the various facets of 'me', identifying them and what part they have played and continue to play in my life.  If I can get this far, to allow facets which have been squeezed or have gone unnurtured to  grow and develop whilst acknowledging the well developed primaries..  (see I've already changed the language!!)

 

Having said that, I have a glimmer of an idea for using the approach within my facilitation work but in a very safe way.  I recognise that I do not have Esther’s skill and after Monday realise that primary selves can bring up deep emotions. (yes I experienced lots of 'resonance')

 

I spent the next two days at the IMC conference and there couldn't have been a starker contrast.  A room full of men in suits, bemoaning the recession, rubbing their hands together at the thought of moving into the public sector (after all civil servants haven't got a clue how to deliver services have they) and for whom technology seems to be of paramount importance.  Approaches to people issues were incredibly superficial.

 

One problem I experience is that when I have spent time trying to develop and heighten my interpersonal skills I find it difficult to tolerate colleagues who behave like water buffalo all the time!  I am sorry (not really) to confess that a few of these chaps suffered singed ego's during the two days!  Now I wonder where that self came from!!

 

Please feel free to copy this to the others if you think it appropriate.  My thanks to all for making the day a positive learning event.
