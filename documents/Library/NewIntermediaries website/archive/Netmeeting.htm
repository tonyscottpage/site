To hold a Netmeeting using voice (with video option)…

____________________________________

Netmeeting allows a number of people to "meet" (speaking, seeing, writing on whiteboard, jointly developing a document within an application such as word or powerpoint) over the internet each picking up only one local call charge.

Since this is a new way of working, it might take a few attempts to get going. Follow these instructions but allow time for some "trial and error".

For Windows 95/98 users.

  • Go to the Program Files folder on your hard drive.
  • Open the Netmeeting folder.
  • Click Conf.exe and follow the wizard's instructions to tune in your audio/video. You will need a sound card and microphone. The wizard will tell you whether or not your computer has these features.
  • Log your details into the microsoft business server as instructed.
  • Telephone or send an email to the people you want to meet with, to agree on a time for your Netmeeting.
  • Follow the instructions in the help menu for making making the call to set up your Netmeeting.

If you do not have Windows 95/98, or Netmeeting, then you might have access to other compatible conferencing software. Inquire with your technical support.
