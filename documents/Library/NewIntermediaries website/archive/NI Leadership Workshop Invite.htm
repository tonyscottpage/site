Dear NI

 

Today we face a challenge in NI - perhaps also a great opportunity!

 

You might remember the 9 May stocktaking raised questions about LEADERSHIP in the next phase of NI - could we make the way we operate and decide things "less shadowy and more explicit" . You might also remember that a few weeks ago Frank Smits (frank@symphoenix.net a long standing member of NI) with his colleague Clive Cole (both of them from the Center for Self-Organising Leadership www.centerforselforganizingleadership.com) made a generous offer to facilitate a workshop on self-organising leadership. Now it seems important to take up their offer.

 

Why now?  Because we risk CHAOS and PARALYSIS. Since 9 May stocktake individuals have set off on a series of ACTION STREAMS (eg. experiments in new channels to connect us, collection of workshop wants and offers, guide to NI websites, making links with other networks, opening local NI chapters...) and it might not be apparent but we're at a stage where much more can be initiated by individuals - but progress with each idea and action stream may be BLOCKED for a simple reason. We have no arrangements for taking decisions, coordinating, prioritising. Our continuing growth and vitality (as a community which values open, emergent, potential, energy, self-organising, learning) depends on individuals feeling supported in taking initiatives, so we are inviting you to join others in helping NI take an important step...

 

Why you? We need people who really care about NI to join in a workshop process conducted by Frank and Clive for a full day. To be viable we need 10 or more people. We want firm confirmations: less than 10 and the workshop won't be good so we won't hold it but we can't go much above 20. It's first come first served. The group of 10/20 will work on behalf of the wider NI community. We will consult EVERYONE in NI, before and after, that is, both on the questions we take into the workshop and on the priorities emerging from it. It is up to you to decide how and how much you want to become involved.

 

When? Thursday 28th August. 10am-6pm

 

Where? Hampton Wick Cricket Club. See map at http://www.newintermediaries.co.uk/archive/maphwcc.doc

 

How? We begin with finding A COMPELLING QUESTION. As Frank says this is not a "shoulder shrugger". We "socialise" the question in the wider community before the workshop with the group of 10/20 and bring the workshop outputs to the wider community afterwards to test/challenge/develop. Wider community engagement can happen on email but also through OTHER FORUMS which enable you to connect with other NIers through a different and hotter "real time" medium than email.

 

Notice that we are starting to experiment with Teleforums (with Georgina's help georgina@societyofdreams.com) and Instant Messaging (with Bruce's help im4xlns@hotmail.com). You can initiate and respond to Teleforum/Instant Messaging/Email conversations at any time in this process before and after the workshop. Please bear iwth us on this: we might not get mix of modalities totally right but don't doubt that we will be continuing to learn about how we can operate as an engaged Community of Practice - by the way we have another exciting event on this coming shortly too.

 

What's the compelling question? We've considered several specific questions such as what we most value in NI, what makes our wheels grip or spin,  what we find exciting, what life's trying to happen, how to enable initiatives, coordinate, etc. But we'll start with a broad, umbrella question that can enable more specific points to emerge: "How can we make NI a success for all involved?" and see where this takes us.

 

What will we produce?  We will produce a map and 4-6 priorities to take NI forward.

 

For now, all I need from you is to know whether you can attend the 28 August workshop. Please email me tony@pageconsulting.co.uk with confirmation that you can attend, and/or with any questions.

 

Frank and I look forward to your participation.

 

Tony
