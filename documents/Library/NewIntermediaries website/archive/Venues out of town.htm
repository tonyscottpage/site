Different Venues inc out of town

 

 

  • Mike Straw who has access to a wonderful place.  It is about 1 hour down the M4.  If you are interested call 01453 861 050 (Richard Cooke)

 

  • Have you considered the Earth Centre in Doncaster? That has a great conference facility and a little hotel to support it.  Definitely not polished. http://www.earthcentre.org.uk/ Had a great workshop with a client there last year where we planted 800 odd trees for them as part of it. (Jeremy Keeley)

 

  • Otherwise you could try the All Saints Pastoral Centre in London Colney near St Albans...I use that for my Sadler Heath events. http://www.hertsdirect.org/infoadvice It's definitely not conferency/hotely. (Jeremy Keeley)

 

  • Kew Gardens? (Iain Carruthers)

 

  • I have always wanted to use Bramble Park for a workshop, an unusual B & B in Trottiscliffe near Maidstone, nearest station Borough Green, with fabulous grounds and a very large second lounge. Sleeps 10 max in 6 rooms. Used to be the Rectory now owned by German lady Renata Towler who loves cooking and is sociable without being invasive. 01732 822397. Have spent many a wonderful weekend there alone with the trees getting away from it all.(Julia Williams)

 

  • And there is always Regents Park College. Pretty booked up but close to Baker St and is always light. (Julia Williams)

 

  • You might try Bore Place near Chiddingstone aka Commonwork. It's a converted manor house in its own 500 acre working farm with its own brickworks from local clay. Plenty of nature to go traipsing through. (Nic Shugar)
