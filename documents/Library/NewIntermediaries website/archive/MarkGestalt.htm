Dear Tony and Julie

Thank you both again for your time in preparing and running tuesday's event, which I really enjoyed.

I thought the 'House of Change' was a great exercise, usable at many levels, and fascinating to link in the interruptions to contact with each 'room'. I think the freedom that you gave us allowed everyone to bring all their energies to bear on the subject.

The day brought many insights, new and resonant :

  • The unspoken, non rational is what often drives organisations - how do we get it out ?
  • Levels of awareness - sometimes we can describe all that we are 'aware' of,and still miss out other feelings somehow in the background, on a different level. I guess gestalt is moving towards a wider awareness of all those levels.
  • The importance of recontracting levels of authority when facilitating.
  • The value in organisational research of ensuring that clients/ managers have at least some first hand experience of the issues/ problems (as customers?), and that it's not all reports and stats.
  • Fundamentally what we do is offer support to clients in moving on to the next stage of change.
  • 'Should' often means introjection.
  • The empty zone of change frequently lasts a while.
  • Being too controlled (or empathetic) when facilitating/ consulting may be inauthentic, and seen as insincere and manipulative. Showing that you too are human can build trust.
  • When running a workshop, be as open as you can about your plan for the day.
  • When consulting be as open about your remit and agenda as you can. People may not like it, but they are more likely to trust you.
  • Slow it down - draw the full meaning and value out.

Really enjoyed meeting the group - as passionate about their subject/s as they are mixed ! Great venue too - really different from the norm and therefore easier to step away from work and reflect.

Hope to see you at another NI event before too long.
