Insider-outsider dialogue

Our work as 'intermediaries' is very much tied up with crossing boundaries (eg. internal/external change agents, bricks/clicks, leaders/luddites) to create new knowledge and understanding, primarily through face to face contact and two way exchange. We also seek to gather new perspectives on change from other communities; theatre, arts, therapy, and other kinds of intermediaries.

As an early example of being 'intermediaries' we identified that this community serves both internal and external consultants, and we held two events specifically to bring these groups into dialogue with one another. It became clear that we have a common agenda and dislike being boxed as labelled as 'insiders' or 'outsiders', but also that these two groups do have different needs: external consultants need contact with others to avoid isolation, internal consultants gain value from a distancing and perspective on their own home cultures.

This experiment on ourselves reflects our role as intermediaries in our orgaisational consulting work through which we build teams, help resolve conflict, mobilise people for change. In this work we are:

MEDIATING between usually separate knowledge spaces and cultures
BROKERING CONVERSATIONS for learning, and self-sustaining change.
